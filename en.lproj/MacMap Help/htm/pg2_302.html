Print Area Tool
Use

The Print Area tool lets you define the print boundaries for published documents (Publish as PDF, Publish as KML, Publish as JPEG, Publish as TIFF, Publish as PNG, Print). A click and drag on the document lets you define the area. It is indicated by a dashed, black border.

Popup

The popup gives you access to 4 functions:
Center: center the print area on the center of the window.
Fit to Window: adapt the print area to the size of the window.
Adapt to Selection: adapt the print area to the size of the rectangle encompassing the selection.
Adapt to Contrasts: : adapt the print area to the size of the rectangle encompassing the contrasts.

Options

The options let you specify the size and position of the print area.
The origin of the area is the coordinate of the upper left corner.
The size can be fixed:
Geographic Area: a width and height expressed in the unit of measure for the document.
Size in Pixels: a width and height expressed in pixels.
Specific Paper Size: a width and height expressed in centimeters.
Standard Paper Size: a standard paper size (A4, A3, A5, B5, US Letter or US Legal) and orientation.

You can also choose not to display the print area.
